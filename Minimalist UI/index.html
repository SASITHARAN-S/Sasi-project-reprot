Climate Change Awareness

Understanding the science, impacts, and urgency of our changing climate

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While natural variations occur, scientific evidence shows human activities are the primary driver since the 1800s.

  • Global average temperature has risen 1.1Â°C since pre-industrial times
  • CO2 levels are at their highest in 3 million years
  • Human activities produce 36+ billion tons of CO2 annually

Observable Impacts

Climate change effects are already visible worldwide, affecting ecosystems, weather patterns, and human communities.

  • Sea level rise: 21-24cm since 1880
  • Arctic sea ice declining 13% per decade
  • Increased frequency of extreme weather events
  • Ocean acidification threatening marine life

Taking Action

Addressing climate change requires immediate action at individual, community, and global levels.

  • Transition to renewable energy sources
  • Improve energy efficiency in buildings
  • Adopt sustainable transportation
  • Support climate-friendly policies

The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms Earth's surface. Solar radiation passes through the atmosphere and warms the planet, which then emits infrared radiation back toward space. Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere trap some of this heat, keeping our planet warm enough to support life.

Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

Human activities have increased greenhouse gas concentrations, enhancing the natural greenhouse effect and causing global warming. The main greenhouse gases include:

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO2): 76% of emissions, primarily from fossil fuel burning
  • Methane (CH4): 16% of emissions, from agriculture and waste
  • Nitrous Oxide (N2O): 6% of emissions, from agriculture and industry
  • Fluorinated gases: 2% of emissions, from industrial processes

Sustainability Concepts

Building a future that meets today's needs without compromising tomorrow's possibilities

The Three Pillars

Sustainability rests on three interconnected pillars that must work together for true sustainable development.

  • Environmental: Protecting ecosystems and natural resources
  • Economic: Ensuring prosperity and financial stability
  • Social: Promoting equity, health, and well-being

Circular Economy

Moving away from the linear "take-make-waste" model to a regenerative approach that eliminates waste and maximizes resource use.

  • Design out waste and pollution
  • Keep products and materials in use
  • Regenerate natural systems
  • Powered by renewable energy

Everyday Actions

Small daily choices can make a significant collective impact on sustainability.

  • Reduce, reuse, recycle materials
  • Choose sustainable transportation
  • Support local and ethical businesses
  • Conserve energy and water

Practical Sustainability Tips

At Home

  • Switch to LED light bulbs
  • Unplug electronics when not in use
  • Use programmable thermostats
  • Install low-flow water fixtures

Transportation

  • Walk, bike, or use public transit
  • Carpool or use ride-sharing
  • Consider electric or hybrid vehicles
  • Work from home when possible

Consumption

  • Buy only what you need
  • Choose quality over quantity
  • Support sustainable brands
  • Repair instead of replacing

Renewable Energy

Harnessing nature's power for a clean energy future

Solar Power

Solar energy harnesses sunlight through photovoltaic cells or thermal collectors, providing clean electricity and heating.

  • Fastest-growing energy source globally
  • Costs have dropped 80% since 2010
  • Can power homes, businesses, and utilities
  • Creates jobs in manufacturing and installation

Wind Energy

Wind turbines convert kinetic energy from moving air into electricity, offering scalable clean energy solutions.

  • Second-largest renewable energy source
  • Onshore and offshore installations
  • Can power millions of homes
  • Minimal environmental footprint

Other Renewables

Hydroelectric, geothermal, and biomass energy provide diverse renewable options for different regions and needs.

  • Hydroelectric: Uses flowing water
  • Geothermal: Harnesses Earth's heat
  • Biomass: Converts organic matter
  • Tidal: Captures ocean energy

Environmental Protection

Safeguarding ecosystems and biodiversity for future generations

Biodiversity Conservation

Protecting the variety of life on Earth is crucial for ecosystem stability and human well-being.

  • 1 million species face extinction risk
  • Forests store 1/3 of global CO2 emissions
  • Protected areas cover 15% of land surface
  • Indigenous communities protect 80% of biodiversity

Ocean Health

Oceans regulate climate, provide oxygen, and support billions of people worldwide.

  • Oceans produce 70% of our oxygen
  • 8 million tons of plastic enter oceans annually
  • 30% of CO2 emissions absorbed by oceans
  • Marine protected areas growing globally

Pollution Prevention

Reducing air, water, and soil pollution protects human health and environmental quality.

  • Air pollution causes 7 million deaths annually
  • 2 billion people lack safe drinking water
  • Soil degradation affects 1/3 of farmland
  • Clean technology solutions expanding rapidly
